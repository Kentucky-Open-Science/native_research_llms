# Case studies

Longer looks at individual training runs, where the interesting part isn't the benchmark score but the engineering decision behind it.

These are selected for a specific reason: each one demonstrates something a from-scratch run makes possible that a fine-tune does not. Control over the objective. Control over the tokenizer. Control over the routing architecture. Control over what the model saw and in what order.

The [readme](https://github.com/Kentucky-Open-Science/native_research_llms#readme) tells you a model exists. These pages tell you what its authors had to solve.

## A note on sourcing

Every claim on these pages traces to a public paper, model card, or project page, linked inline. Where a detail is not disclosed, the page says so rather than filling the gap. Where a widely repeated claim doesn't survive checking, the page says that too.
